# FlySF Launch Day Strategy
## Viral Twitter/X Playbook Based on Levelsio & Indie Hacker Best Practices

---

## Executive Summary

Based on research into Levelsio's fly.pieter.com launch (which went from $0 to $1M ARR in 17 days with 320K players) and viral indie game marketing best practices, here's your complete launch day strategy.

**Key Insight from Levelsio**: He did NOT use threads. He posted **separate, standalone tweets** with updates throughout the day/week. Each tweet was its own viral opportunity. He quote-tweeted sparingly, mainly to amplify others talking about his game.

---

## The Levelsio Pattern (What Actually Worked)

### His Posting Structure:
1. **Initial Launch Tweet** - Simple, casual, with video/GIF showing the game
2. **Separate Update Tweets** - NOT a thread, each was standalone with fresh stats/features
3. **Revenue/Stats Updates** - Posted openly with exact numbers
4. **Feature Response Tweets** - "Someone suggested X, so I added it"
5. **Milestone Celebration Tweets** - "Just hit 10K players!" with screenshot

### What He Did NOT Do:
- ‚ùå Long threads explaining how he built it (at launch)
- ‚ùå Quote-tweeting his own posts repeatedly
- ‚ùå Over-explaining or being promotional
- ‚ùå Posting too frequently (burnout/spam)

## Day 2 and Beyond

### The Momentum Loop

Levelsio posted **daily updates** for about 2 weeks, each as a standalone tweet:

1. **Stats updates** (player counts, revenue if monetized)
2. **New features shipped** (based on community suggestions)
3. **Fun moments** (videos of multiplayer chaos, funny bugs)
4. **Milestone celebrations** ($X revenue, Y players, Z feature)

**Frequency:** 2-3 tweets per day MAX. Quality over quantity.

### When to Quote-Tweet Your Own Post

**DO quote-tweet when:**
- Sharing a major milestone (24hr, 48hr stats)
- Someone famous interacts with it
- Adding crucial context/update

**DON'T quote-tweet:**
- Just to bump visibility (looks desperate)
- Multiple times per day
- If the original is underperforming

### When to Use Threads

**Use a thread (later, not launch day) for:**
- "How I built this" technical breakdown
- "Lessons learned" reflection post
- Feature deep-dive

**Thread best practices:**
- Hook in first tweet
- Each tweet should work standalone
- Don't make it too long (5-10 tweets max)
- Post threads in the morning for best reach

## Engagement Strategy

### During Launch Day:

1. **Reply to EVERYONE** who comments positively
   - Short, genuine replies
   - Like their comment too
   - This signals engagement to the algorithm

2. **Don't argue** with critics
   - Thank them for feedback or ignore
   - Arguing kills momentum

3. **Amplify others talking about you**
   - Retweet/quote-tweet people playing your game
   - "Love seeing this!" type responses
   - This encourages more people to post

### The Elon Factor

Levelsio's game exploded partly because Elon Musk retweeted it saying "Wow, this is cool. AI gaming will be massive."

**You can't manufacture this, but you can:**
- Tag relevant people who might genuinely enjoy it
- Make it easy for influential people to discover (gaming hashtags, AI hashtags)
- Have a clearly shareable tweet that others would want to RT

**Do NOT:** Spam influencers. One organic tag in a thread or as a genuine question is fine. Multiple tags = desperation.

## What NOT To Do

### Launch Day Don'ts:

1. ‚ùå **Don't post a thread as your launch**
   - Single tweet performs better
   - Save threads for later breakdowns

2. ‚ùå **Don't over-explain**
   - Let the game speak for itself
   - Curiosity > information

3. ‚ùå **Don't post too frequently**
   - 3-4 posts max on launch day
   - Spamming kills reach per-tweet

4. ‚ùå **Don't be overly promotional**
   - "Check out my amazing game!" = cringe
   - "Made a thing, kinda wild" = authentic

5. ‚ùå **Don't ignore replies**
   - Engagement begets engagement
   - Algorithm rewards conversations

6. ‚ùå **Don't delete underperforming tweets**
   - Looks bad, doesn't help
   - Just let them sit

## The "How I Built This" Thread (Post-Launch)

Do this 2-3 days AFTER launch, once you have traction.

**Structure:**
```
Tweet 1: Hook
"I built a multiplayer flight sim in [X] hours using AI. Here's exactly how:"

Tweet 2-3: The "why"
Why you started, what inspired you

Tweet 4-6: Technical breakdown
Tools used, key decisions, what worked

Tweet 7-8: Challenges
What went wrong, how you fixed it

Tweet 9: Results
Stats, player feedback, surprises

Tweet 10: What's next + CTA
"Try it: [link]"
```

## Metrics to Track

### During Launch Day:
- Impressions per tweet
- Engagement rate (likes + replies + RTs / impressions)
- Click-through rate to your game
- Concurrent players (mention in updates!)

### Signs It's Working:
- Replies from strangers (not just followers)
- Quote-tweets from others
- Follower growth
- External coverage (HN, Reddit, blogs)

### Signs It's Not Working:
- < 1% engagement rate
- No replies from non-followers
- Impressions plateau quickly

**If not working:** Don't panic. Engage with whoever DID respond. Try a different angle tomorrow. Levelsio's success came from consistent shipping + updates, not just the launch tweet.
